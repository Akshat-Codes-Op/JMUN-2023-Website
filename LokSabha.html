Agenda : 1)Reviewing the existing electoral framework with special reference to Anti-Defection Law.
2) Addressing the issue of State control over religious institutions

Almost 75 years since independence, and some grave issues still plague the world’s largest democratic exercise- Indian elections. With immorality and hypocrisy creeping into the system of elections, fair representation of the people in the legislatures seems to be a pipe-dream. As the contentions over freebies and party-hopping grow, the need to review the Anti-Defection Law, among other things, is greater than ever. India identifies as a secular nation in the Preamble of the Constitution. However, the governments in several states seem to control the management of religious institutions of a particular faith. While some argue for the autonomy of the temples, others see this demand as a bid to divide this country by flaring up communalism. We leave it to you, Parliamentarians, to reflect on the integrity of our Constitution and decide the path ahead.

Lok Sabha (सत्यमेव जयते)
